# Task: MPE / zero-person — confirmation or threat?

**Status:** OPEN — decision required; determines whether the Sandved-Smith
et al. paper is the Credo's best evidence or its hardest objection.
**Origin:** "There is no self-evidence" (Sandved-Smith, Fields, Doctor,
Laukkonen, Hohwy, 2026) intersection.
**Depends on:** `constitutive_vs_about.md`, `phenomenal_variation.md`

## The collision
Same machinery, opposite terminus. The Credo: drive the reflexive loop to
its fixed point → selfhood (credo ergo sum). That paper: model your own
reflexive (QRF) dynamics → discover the self/world boundary is an
unevidenceable prior σ → prune it (Bayesian model reduction). One theory's
destination is the other's avidyā.

## The decision
Their post-dual agent keeps self-evidencing *unimpaired* after σ is pruned.
So something persists through the dissolution. Two readings:
- **Confirmation:** σ is an *about-layer* belief in separation; what
  persists is the *constitutive* reflexive loop. Minimal Phenomenal
  Experience / zero-person = the fixed point running with one content-belief
  removed → experience without selfhood is exactly what the belief/experience
  split predicts. Strongest possible support for the constitutive/about
  distinction.
- **Threat:** the self they prune *is* the self the Credo constitutes;
  "there is no self-evidence" negates "credo ergo sum" directly.

## What resolves it
Commit to the first reading and defend it: show MPE retains reflexive
closure (still something it is like) while losing only the σ content.
The defence stands or falls with `fixed_point_frame_relativity.md` — if the
fixed point is only frame-relative, their prune reaches it too.

## First move
State precisely what is left when σ is gone, in Credo terms, and check it
contains the fixed-point structure and not merely "function continues."
