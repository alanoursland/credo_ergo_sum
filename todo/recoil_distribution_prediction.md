# Task: The recoil-distribution commitment

**Status:** OPEN — a tension *within* the framework to reconcile, plus a
testable prediction to accept or decline.
**Origin:** Tension between `objection_illusionist_fork.md` §IV and the
neurodiversity-of-illusionism claim in `phenomenal_variation.md` §V.
**Depends on:** `objection_illusionist_fork.md`, `phenomenal_variation.md`

## The tension
- §IV (Frankish fork) uses illusionists' *recoil* as evidence: denial of
  experience is "assertable but not occupiable," and the literature's shape
  (denial in theory, perpetual concession that things "really seem") is the
  predicted instability. This requires recoiling illusionists to *have* the
  fixed point and fail to occupy their denial.
- Neurodiversity says some illusionists *genuinely lack* the fixed point and
  report accurately (no recoil).

You cannot have both for the same person.

## The reconciliation (a new, testable commitment)
The population splits: most have the fixed point and recoil; a tail
genuinely differs and does not; committed illusionist philosophers are
enriched *from that tail*. This predicts something sharp and falsifiable:
**committed illusionists should show *less* denial-recoil than average, not
the same.** The §IV recoil-as-evidence claim must then be scoped to *the
people who recoil*, not asserted universally.

## The decision
Accept this prediction and commit (it sharpens the theory and is empirically
exposed), or find another reconciliation. The framework's discipline
(`constitutive_vs_about.md`) bars inferring any *individual's* structure
from their professed philosophy — keep the claim about populations and
measurable structure.

## First move
State the predicted recoil distribution explicitly and check it against the
direction §IV currently leans (which points the opposite way).
