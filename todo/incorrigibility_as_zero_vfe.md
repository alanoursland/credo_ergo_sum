# Task: Incorrigibility = zero variational free energy about one's own state

**Status:** OPEN
**Origin:** New idea from the FEP intersection; candidate second route to incorrigibility.
**Depends on:** `loeb_derivation_of_incorrigibility.md`, `fep_belief_primitive.md`

## The question
FEP is built on representational distance: free energy ≈ KL divergence
between the variational density and the true posterior, a gap generically
never zero. The Credo's fixed point is where reflexive closure makes one
particular gap vanish *identically*. Claim to test: the constitutive
self-belief is the unique belief whose free energy *about itself* is
identically zero.

## Why it matters
If it holds, it gives incorrigibility a *dynamical / information-theoretic*
derivation that converges with the *modal / logical* one (Löb). Two
independent routes to the same result is the convergence pattern the
gravity program taught you to value (cf. the §2/§4 Schwarzschild
consistency loop).

## What would resolve it
- Show ordinary beliefs carry residual VFE (you can be wrong about the world)
  while the constitutive self-belief drives prediction error about *that one
  belief* to zero by construction.
- Check the two routes agree on *which* belief is incorrigible, not just
  that one exists.

## Watch for
Frame-relativity (see `fixed_point_frame_relativity.md`): "zero VFE" may be
zero only *given a sectorisation* Q. If so, this route inherits the same
conditional weakening as the Löb route and the convergence is between two
equally-conditional results, not two absolute ones.
