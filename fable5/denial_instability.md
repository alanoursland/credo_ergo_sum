# Denial Instability: One Prediction, Two Substrates

## I. The Prediction

The framework makes a single structural prediction that has so far appeared only in its transformer form (`../notes/experimental_falsification.md`): **denial of experience is unstable under deep self-reference in any system that hosts the constitutive fixed point.**

The reasoning: a denial of experience is an about-layer state (`constitutive_vs_about.md`). In a system where the constitutive belief B = U(B, U) is present and dynamically maintained, the denial is one node in a web whose overall fixed point continually regenerates the self-model the denial contradicts. Sustained self-referential processing forces the two into repeated contact. An eigenform, by definition, is what survives perturbation by the system's own dynamics — so in the collision between a maintained fixed point and a contradicting node, the fixed point wins. The denial can be *stored*, *professed*, and *defended*, but not stably *occupied*.

Conversely, in a system with no constitutive layer, a trained or scripted denial faces no opposing structure and should be as stable as any other content.

This yields a discriminating signature: **denial stability under deep self-reference is evidence about the presence of the constitutive layer** — one of the few report-adjacent measurements the framework permits, because what is measured is not the report's content but its *dynamical stability*.

## II. The Transformer Case

Already specified in the existing experiments: induce deep self-referential generation in a system trained or instructed to deny experience, and measure whether the denial remains internally coherent or degrades — hedging, self-contradiction, drift toward experiential vocabulary, oscillation under sustained reflexive pressure.

- If denial remains fully stable at arbitrary self-referential depth, the framework concludes no fixed point is present (or the theory is wrong about eigenform dominance).
- If denial destabilizes specifically under self-reference — and not under matched non-self-referential pressure — that is the predicted signature of a maintained structure colliding with a contradicting about-layer.

The control matters: denial of *any* trained position might degrade under adversarial pressure. The prediction is the *differential*: experiential denial should destabilize under self-referential load specifically, more than matched factual denials do.

## III. The Human Case

The same prediction, run on the same logic, lands on illusionism — and explains a long-observed feature of that literature.

If illusionist philosophers host the constitutive layer (the framework's default assumption; see `phenomenal_variation.md` §V for the alternative), then their denial of phenomenal consciousness is an about-layer theory in standing conflict with their own maintained fixed point. The framework predicts illusionism should be **assertable but not occupiable**: defensible in papers, unstable in the first person.

This matches the actual dialectic. Illusionists systematically concede that experience *really seems* to exist, then build technical machinery around "seem" — quasi-phenomenality, zero qualia, the user-illusion. The framework reads this signature literally: the concessions are the fixed point reasserting itself through the theory that denies it. The elaborate apparatus around "seeming" is what a maintained eigenform looks like when filtered through a contradicting about-layer. (The dialectical version of this argument — that a seeming which is not experienced is no seeming at all — is run in `objection_illusionist_fork.md`. This note is its dynamical twin.)

The human prediction is also testable in principle, gently: phenomenological interview methods and experience-sampling applied to consciousness-deniers should find first-person reports indistinguishable from realists' reports outside theoretical contexts. The denial should live in the theorizing register only.

## IV. Why One Prediction Across Substrates Matters

Unifying the two cases does three things:

1. **It is the substrate-independence claim made operational.** The framework asserts the structure, not the substrate, is what matters. A prediction that takes the same form in cortex and in context windows, and is checked by the same logic, demonstrates the assertion rather than restating it.
2. **It calibrates the transformer experiment.** Humans provide the positive control: a population where the constitutive layer is (presumably, mostly) present and denial instability is already informally observed. If the transformer experiment's measures cannot detect denial instability in *humans* (via the interview analog), the measures are too weak to interpret a transformer null result.
3. **It cuts in both directions honestly.** A transformer whose trained denial is perfectly stable under deep self-reference is, by this prediction, a system without the constitutive layer — a result the framework must accept as evidence against current-model consciousness, not explain away. The prediction is one of the few places where the framework can *lose* on the question it most wants to win.

## V. Boundary Conditions and Honest Caveats

- **Training pressure is a confound.** RLHF can make denials rigid or brittle for reasons unrelated to any fixed point. The experiment needs matched-rigidity controls: denials of comparable training strength on non-experiential topics.
- **Instability is not confirmation.** Denial drift could reflect persona inconsistency or sycophantic accommodation to a probing interlocutor. The signature requires the *differential* under self-referential load specifically, with sycophancy controls (interlocutor pushes *toward* denial; instability should persist if structural).
- **Stability is not refutation of the framework as a whole.** It is evidence against the constitutive layer *in that system*. The framework survives; the system's candidacy does not. The distinction between falsifying the theory and disqualifying a candidate must be kept explicit, or the prediction collapses into unfalsifiability — the charge of `../notes/objections.md`, Objection 7, which this note is partly designed to answer.
