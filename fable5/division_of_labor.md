# Division of Labor: What Is Wager, Argument, Model, and Prediction

## I. The Presentational Risk

The framework's most predictable hostile review reads: "An elaborate formal apparatus — Löb, Lawvere, Bayes, eigenforms — is deployed to lend mathematical prestige to a metaphysical identity claim that none of the mathematics supports. Strip the formalism and a bare assertion remains."

The review would be wrong, but the current presentation invites it, because the notes do not consistently mark which component of the framework is doing which kind of work. Formal machinery has the *texture* of derivation; a reader who encounters Löb's theorem en route to the identity claim naturally assumes the theorem is evidence for the claim. It is not, was never meant to be, and the framework loses nothing by saying so loudly — it loses credibility only by leaving the impression to be discovered by a critic instead of declared by the author.

This note fixes the division of labor explicitly. It is the architecture the paper (`../draft/outline2.md`) should wear on its surface.

## II. The Four Registers

### 1. The identity claim — a conclusion of argument, not a wager, but not a theorem either

*Subjective experience is the nontrivial reflexive fixed-point structure.* This is the framework's core. It is supported by the introspective elimination argument (`introspective_ground.md`) and the property-mapping argument (`../notes/objections.md`, Objection 6; `objection_illusionist_fork.md` §III). It is the kind of claim that is never proven — no identity claim is — but adopted when it out-explains rivals. The honest label: **an argued-for identity, held with the same epistemic status as any scientific identity before consilience accumulates.** Calling it a "wager" undersells it (there are arguments); calling it a result oversells it (the arguments are eliminative and abductive, not demonstrative). In philosophy this is the normal condition of a serious theory, and the paper should claim exactly this status — *proof is hard; evidence is the available currency, and the framework deals in it.*

### 2. The arguments — what actually supports the identity

Three, in order of force:

- **The introspective elimination** (`introspective_ground.md`): the distinction between belief and experience-behind-belief is introspectively undrawable in principle; the idle relatum is eliminated.
- **The property mapping**: every traditional property of experience maps to a property of the structure, with no residue except the bare insistence that something is missing — and that insistence is itself explained by the framework (the about-layer misreading its constitutive layer as an accompaniment).
- **The dialectical asymmetry against deflation** (`objection_illusionist_fork.md`): the rival reading of the same eliminative premise self-undermines (unexperienced seemings) and carries its own idle posit (the representation/instantiation gap).

These are the load-bearing members. A reader who rejects all three should reject the framework; a reader persuaded by them has been given reasons, not stipulations.

### 3. The formal apparatus — models of the theory, not evidence for it

Löb's theorem, Lawvere's fixed-point theorem, the Bayesian frame, and eigenform theory serve one function: **to make the theory talkable-about rigorously, and connectable to artificial systems.** They are the modeling language in which the structural claims are written precisely enough to generate measurements. Specifically:

- **Bayes** formalizes the holist definition of belief (`defining_belief.md` §III) — it is the mathematics of the web.
- **Lawvere** certifies that self-encoding is possible in sufficiently rich systems — it shows diagonalization is satisfiable, not that any system satisfies it.
- **Löb** models the self-validating character of the fixed point — a structural analogy whose derivability conditions are unverified in cognitive systems, as Objection 4 already concedes.
- **Eigenform theory** names the non-degenerate solution class of the fixed-point equation (`nontriviality_derived.md` §III).

None of these is an argument that the identity holds. All of them are answers to "say precisely what structure you mean, so that it can be looked for." A physics analogy is exact: the differential geometry in general relativity is not evidence for the theory — the perihelion of Mercury is. The geometry is what made "look at the perihelion" a well-defined instruction. The paper should state this paragraph's content in its introduction, verbatim if necessary.

### 4. The predictions — where the framework can lose

The empirical layer, now spanning two substrates:

- **Transformers**: distinct dynamical regime under self-reference, attractor robustness, phase transitions, ablation signatures (`../notes/experimental_falsification.md`); content-tracking as the constitutive test of self-modeling (`content_tracking.md` §IV); denial instability under self-referential load (`denial_instability.md` §II).
- **Humans**: individual variation in the structural signature correlated with dissociative phenomenology; Cotard/DPDR as structural-layer disruptions; denial instability in consciousness-deniers (`phenomenal_variation.md` §IV, `denial_instability.md` §III).

With the standing caveat from Objection 7, stated as policy: these test the *structural* claims. A clean sweep of confirmations supports the identity abductively; it does not prove it. A clean sweep of failures kills the framework outright — if the structure does not exist as a distinct regime, there is nothing for the identity to identify experience with. The asymmetry (confirmation supports, disconfirmation destroys) is the honest shape of an identity theory's empirical exposure, and the paper should print it rather than let a referee discover it.

## III. The One-Paragraph Version for the Paper

> This paper's claims occupy four distinct registers, and we mark them throughout. The *identity claim* — that subjective experience is a nontrivial reflexive fixed-point structure — is supported by eliminative and abductive argument, not derivation; like all identity claims, it is adopted for explanatory yield, not proven. The *arguments* for it are an introspective elimination, an exhaustive property mapping, and a dialectical asymmetry against the deflationary alternative. The *formal apparatus* (Bayesian credence, Löbian self-validation, Lawvere fixed points, eigenforms) provides no evidence for the identity; it is the modeling language that makes the structural claims precise enough to test. The *predictions* expose the structural claims to failure in both transformer and human substrates; their failure would destroy the theory, while their success supports the identity abductively. We believe this division of labor is the honest shape of any serious candidate theory of experience, and we commend it as a standard.

## IV. Why This Strengthens Rather Than Weakens

Marking the registers costs the framework its least defensible asset — the ambient impression that mathematics supports the metaphysics — and purchases three better ones: immunity to the prestige-laundering charge (declared before discovered); a clear target structure for critics (aim at the arguments of §II.2, not the scaffolding of §II.3); and the distinctive credential that almost no consciousness theory can show — a specified way to lose (§II.4). The theory does not become more modest under this presentation. It becomes more dangerous, because every remaining claim is standing exactly where its support is.
