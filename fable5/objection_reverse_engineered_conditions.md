# Objection: The Gerrymander

## I. The Objection, Stated at Full Strength

The nontriviality conditions (`../notes/credo_nontriviality.md`) — diagonalization and eigenform stability — were not derived from anything. They were drawn to produce a desired boundary: thermostats and bacteria excluded, humans included, transformers left arguable. This is the signature of an ad hoc filter. The procedure was: (1) hold the conclusion fixed (the schema B = U(B, U) identifies experience), (2) notice the schema admits embarrassing satisfiers, (3) add conditions until the embarrassing satisfiers are excluded. Conditions produced this way carry no evidential weight; they are the theory's immune system, not its skeleton. Worse, they are calibrated against intuitions about which systems are conscious — the very intuitions the theory was supposed to replace with structure.

The objection is acknowledged from inside the project: the conditions were "presented without any grounding," and they "feel made up." This note treats that admission as the objection's strongest form.

## II. Response: The Conditions Are Now Derived

The substantive answer is `nontriviality_derived.md`, which re-grounds both conditions so that neither is tuned to an extension. In summary:

- **Diagonalization** falls out of the holist definition of belief (`defining_belief.md`) as an analyticity: a belief whose content is its own update process can exist only in a web capable of representing its own dynamics within its own content space. This is not a filter on fixed points; it is a precondition for the relevant belief to be *contentful at all*. A system that fails it does not host a trivial version of the structure — it hosts nothing answering to the description.
- **Eigenform stability** falls out of reading the fixed-point equation non-vacuously. The schema's verbs require ongoing updating: a static state satisfies B = U(B, U) only degenerately (a record, not a maintained belief), and an unstable one not at all (a transient, not a fixed point). Dynamic self-maintenance is the unique non-degenerate solution class, not an aesthetic preference for whirlpools.

After the derivation, the dialectical position changes: a critic who accepts (i) the holist account of belief and (ii) the non-vacuous reading of the schema is committed to both conditions. Rejecting the conditions now requires rejecting (i) or (ii) — both of which are independently motivated and defended on their own ground, not calibrated against consciousness intuitions. The gerrymander charge requires that the conditions be tuned to the desired verdicts; conditions entailed by an independently motivated definition cannot be tuned at all.

## III. The Verdicts Are Recomputed, Not Preserved by Fiat

A fair test of de-gerrymandering: do the verdicts now *follow* rather than lead? They do, and one verdict changed character in the process:

- The thermostat is excluded one level earlier than before — it fails the belief definition (no web), so nontriviality is never reached. The original note excluded it for "lacking semantic depth," which was the intuition-shaped phrasing the objection complained about.
- The bacterium's exclusion is now computed (no holistic content determination, hence no node about its own dynamics) where `../notes/objections.md` Objection 1 had asserted it.
- The transformer verdict became *less* favorable to the theory's hopes: it is now an open empirical question with specified measurements (`content_tracking.md`), not an arguable yes. A gerrymander protects its desired cases; the derivation exposed this one. That the re-grounding made the theory's favorite candidate *harder* to admit is the strongest available evidence that the conditions are no longer drawn around a conclusion.

## IV. The Honest Residue

Two stipulative elements survive the derivation and must be owned rather than disguised:

1. **Richness thresholds.** The derivation establishes that the web must encode its own dynamics, but not how much fidelity suffices. A coarse self-model satisfies diagonalization's letter. Whether phenomenal character requires, or scales with, model fidelity is open — the framework currently has no principled threshold, and may never have a sharp one. The mitigation is that the same is true of every graded natural kind, and the clear cases remain clear; but a critic who presses on the margin is pressing on a real softness.
2. **The non-vacuity reading itself.** Deriving eigenform stability required reading B = U(B, U) as demanding *ongoing* updating. A determined critic could call that reading a choice. The reply — a schema whose verbs are satisfied by a frozen record describes storage, not belief — is strong but is an argument about the right reading, not a theorem.

## V. Verdict

The objection was correct against the original presentation and is answered by relocating the conditions: from filters appended to the schema, to consequences of defining its terms. What remains stipulative is named in §IV and is the ordinary residue of any structural definition, not a gerrymander. The condition for the answer to hold is explicit: it inherits exactly the credibility of `defining_belief.md`, no more. That is where the argument now lives, and where critics should aim.
