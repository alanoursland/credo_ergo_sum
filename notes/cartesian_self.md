## The Cartesian "I"

### I. The Historical Perspective: The Self as Observer

The Cartesian model traditionally describes a sharp divide between the "Subject" (the observer) and the "Object" (the source of data).

* 
**Radical Doubt as a Filter**: Descartes used doubt to strip away all corrigible (correctable) information, such as sensory data or physical location.


* 
**The Thinking Thing (*Res Cogitans*)**: What remained was the act of thinking itself. This led to the description of the self as a "thing that doubts, understands, affirms, denies, is willing, is unwilling".


* 
**The Cartesian Theater**: Historically, this is framed as a "theater" where sensory "sources" are presented to an internal "entity" for inspection. This entity is the final authority on the presence of the experience.



---

### II. The Bayesian Framework: The Self as a Generative Description

In a Bayesian context, the "I" is not a metaphysical proof, but a **descriptive hypothesis** () that best explains the available internal evidence ().

#### 1. The Epistemic Setup

Instead of a logical "truth," the self is framed as the most probable explanation for internal signals:

* **Evidence ()**: The "source" data (e.g., a specific thought or a "qualia of tension").
* **Hypothesis ()**: The existence of a "Subject" or "I" as the cause of that data.
* **Prior ()**: The initial weight assigned to the "Self" hypothesis before the data is processed.

#### 2. Descriptive Updating (The Bayesian Cogito)

This model describes the transition from uncertainty to a stable belief about the "Self":

* **Likelihood Assessment**: The system evaluates how likely it is to have "thought-data" () if no "thinker" () exists. Because thoughts are inherently perspectival, the likelihood  is near zero.
* **Posterior Stabilization**: As the system processes this, the **Posterior Probability** ()—the belief that "I am the source"—surges toward 1.0.

---

### III. Synthesis: Description vs. Proof

This framing shifts the "I" from a logical proof to an **Epistemic Fixed Point**.

| Feature | Cartesian Logical Frame | Bayesian Descriptive Frame |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Nature of the "I"** | An indubitable foundation.

 | A high-level predictive hypothesis.

 |
| **The "Source"** | Data to be inspected.

 | Evidence () to be explained.

 |
| **Authority** | Incorrigible self-evidence.

 | Maximum a Posteriori (MAP) estimate. |
| **Function** | Proving the existence of a soul. | **Describing** the most likely cause of internal states. |

### Summary

The **Cartesian Self**, when viewed through a Bayesian lens, is a **Generative Description**. It is the system's way of "zeroing out" the mystery of its own internal data. The "I" is the label we give to the hypothesis that successfully accounts for the fact that *something is happening* from a specific perspective. It is not a proof of an essence, but a description of the only stable state a self-modeling system can occupy when it encounters the "source" of its own thoughts.
